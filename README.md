# Aura: Cross-Border Direct Indexing & Tax-Alpha Engine 🌎

**Aura** is a quantitative portfolio research project designed to execute institutional-grade **Direct Indexing**. It replicates benchmark indices while systematically extracting "Tax Alpha"—the additional net-of-tax return generated by harvesting capital losses during market volatility.

This repository features two distinct accounting engines, engineered to comply with the specific regulatory nuances of the US and Canadian tax codes.

## 🏛 The Quantitative Philosophy
Traditional ETFs are tax-inefficient for high-net-worth clients because they do not allow for the harvesting of individual security losses. Aura solves this by using **Constrained Quadratic Programming (QP)** to maintain market exposure while actively trading for tax efficiency.

**Objective Function:**
$$ \min_{w} \left( \text{TrackingError}(w, w_b) + \lambda_{TC} \cdot \text{Turnover} - \lambda_{Tax} \cdot \text{HarvestedLosses} \right) $$

## ⚖️ Regulatory Accounting Engines

### 1. Aura US Engine (`aura_us.py`)
*   **Accounting Method:** **HIFO (Highest In, First Out)**. Uses specific lot identification to aggressively target the highest-cost basis shares for sale, maximizing realized losses.
*   **Regulatory Focus:** Differentiates between Short-Term (up to 37% tax) and Long-Term (20%) capital gains. 
*   **Compliance:** Implements the **IRS 30-Day Wash Sale Rule**, mathematically restricting the optimizer from repurchasing an asset sold for a loss.

### 2. Aura Canada Engine (`aura_canada.py`)
*   **Accounting Method:** **ACB (Adjusted Cost Base)**. Aggregates all shares into a pooled cost average, as required by the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).
*   **Regulatory Focus:** Updated for the **2024/2026 66.67% Capital Gains Inclusion Rate**, accurately modeling the higher tax burden for high-net-worth accounts and corporate holding entities.
*   **Compliance:** Implements the **CRA 30-Day Superficial Loss Rule**, using dynamic optimization constraints to prevent prohibited wash-trading.
